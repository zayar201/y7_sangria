What is HTML?

HTML, or Hypertext Markup Language, is the basic language used to create and organize content on websites. It allows web developers to structure text, images, links, and other elements on a page. Together with CSS, which controls the design, and JavaScript, which adds interactive features, HTML makes up the foundation of most modern websites. It tells the browser how to display content like headings, paragraphs, lists, and so much more.

HTML is a great starting point for anyone new to coding. In fact, it is among the easiest to learn! It's very simple and easy, which is why it’s often the first language that beginners, including young learners, try. By understanding HTML, people can begin creating their own websites and get a hands-on introduction to the world of programming. It’s a skill that can lead to creativity, problem-solving, and even future career opportunities in technology.
